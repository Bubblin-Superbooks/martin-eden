At his command, “Bring in the clan,” Andy departed to go the round of the rooms for the lodgers.

“We’re lucky that most of them are here,” Brissenden whispered to Martin.  “There’s Norton and Hamilton; come on and meet them.  Stevens isn’t around, I hear.  I’m going to get them started on monism if I can.  Wait till they get a few jolts in them and they’ll warm up.”

At first the conversation was desultory.  Nevertheless Martin could not fail to appreciate the keen play of their minds.  They were men with opinions, though the opinions often clashed, and, though they were witty and clever, they were not superficial.  He swiftly saw, no matter upon what they talked, that each man applied the correlation of knowledge and had also a deep-seated and unified conception of society and the Cosmos.
