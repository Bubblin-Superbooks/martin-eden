The bed, against the rear wall, occupied two-thirds of the total space of the room.  The table was flanked on one side by a gaudy bureau, manufactured for profit and not for service, the thin veneer of which was shed day by day.  This bureau stood in the corner, and in the opposite corner, on the table’s other flank, was the kitchen—the oil-stove on a dry-goods box, inside of which were dishes and cooking utensils, a shelf on the wall for provisions, and a bucket of water on the floor.  Martin had to carry his water from the kitchen sink, there being no tap in his room.  On days when there was much steam to his cooking, the harvest of veneer from the bureau was unusually generous.  Over the bed, hoisted by a tackle to the ceiling, was his bicycle.
