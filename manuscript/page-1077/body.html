“All right, then,” Martin said.  “I’ll pay the thirty-five a month, and—”

He reached across the table for the check.  But Bernard Higginbotham got his hand on it first, crying:

“I accept!  I accept!”

When Martin got on the electric car, he was very sick and tired.  He looked up at the assertive sign.

“The swine,” he groaned.  “The swine, the swine.”

When Mackintosh’s Magazine published “The Palmist,” featuring it with decorations by Berthier and with two pictures by Wenn, Hermann von Schmidt forgot that he had called the verses obscene.  He announced that his wife had inspired the poem, saw to it that the news reached the ears of a reporter, and submitted to an interview by a staff writer who was accompanied by a staff photographer and a staff artist.
