He paused for a moment, and watched the youthful shade of himself, in stiff-rim and square-cut, enter the door and swagger across the room.  “As I was saying, up here I thought all men and women were brilliant and radiant.  But now, from what little I’ve seen of them, they strike me as a pack of ninnies, most of them, and ninety percent of the remainder as bores.  Now there’s Professor Caldwell—he’s different.  He’s a man, every inch of him and every atom of his gray matter.”

Ruth’s face brightened.

“Tell me about him,” she urged.  “Not what is large and brilliant—I know those qualities; but whatever you feel is adverse.  I am most curious to know.”
