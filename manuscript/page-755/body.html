“If I fail?”  He regarded her for a moment as though the thought she had uttered was unthinkable.  Then intelligence illumined his eyes.  “If I fail, I shall become an editor, and you will be an editor’s wife.”

She frowned at his facetiousness—a pretty, adorable frown that made him put his arm around her and kiss it away.

“There, that’s enough,” she urged, by an effort of will withdrawing herself from the fascination of his strength.  “I have talked with father and mother.  I never before asserted myself so against them.  I demanded to be heard.  I was very undutiful.  They are against you, you know; but I assured them over and over of my abiding love for you, and at last father agreed that if you wanted to, you could begin right away in his office.
