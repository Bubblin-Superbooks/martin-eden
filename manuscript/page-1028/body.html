He offered her an extraneous thing with which he could part without a pang, while she offered him herself, along with disgrace and shame, and sin, and all her hopes of heaven.

“Don’t let’s talk about it,” she said with a catch in her voice that she changed to a cough.  She stood up.  “Come on, let’s go home.  I’m all tired out.”

The day was done, and the merrymakers had nearly all departed.  But as Martin and Lizzie emerged from the trees they found the gang waiting for them.  Martin knew immediately the meaning of it.  Trouble was brewing.  The gang was his body-guard.  They passed out through the gates of the park with, straggling in the rear, a second gang, the friends that Lizzie’s young man had collected to avenge the loss of his lady.
