“But music, you know, is a matter of training,” Ruth argued; “and opera is even more a matter of training.  May it not be—”

“That I am not trained in opera?” he dashed in.

She nodded.

“The very thing,” he agreed.  “And I consider I am fortunate in not having been caught when I was young.  If I had, I could have wept sentimental tears to-night, and the clownish antics of that precious pair would have but enhanced the beauty of their voices and the beauty of the accompanying orchestra.  You are right.  It’s mostly a matter of training.  And I am too old, now.  I must have the real or nothing.  An illusion that won’t convince is a palpable lie, and that’
