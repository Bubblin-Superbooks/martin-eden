He wished Ruth was there to share in his joy, and when he went over the letters left lying on his bed, he found one from her.  It was sweetly reproachful, wondering what had kept him away for so dreadful a length of time.  He reread the letter adoringly, dwelling over her handwriting, loving each stroke of her pen, and in the end kissing her signature.

And when he answered, he told her recklessly that he had not been to see her because his best clothes were in pawn.  He told her that he had been sick, but was once more nearly well, and that inside ten days or two weeks (as soon as a letter could travel to New York City and return) he would redeem his clothes and be with her.
