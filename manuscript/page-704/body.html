To cap the situation, “The Pot,” which he looked upon as one of the best things he had written, was lost to him.  In despair, casting about frantically among the magazines, he had sent it to The Billow, a society weekly in San Francisco.  His chief reason for submitting it to that publication was that, having only to travel across the bay from Oakland, a quick decision could be reached.  Two weeks later he was overjoyed to see, in the latest number on the news-stand, his story printed in full, illustrated, and in the place of honor.  He went home with leaping pulse, wondering how much they would pay him for one of the best things he had done.  Also, the celerity with which it had been accepted and published was a pleasant thought to him.  That the editor had not informed him of the acceptance made the surprise more complete.
